A Holistic Approach to Education for High School Students

Holistic education has been making the headlines for a long time now; however, it seems more in the context of early childhood education than in the context of formal school education. A holistic approach to education is as important for grown-up children as it was important for them during their childhood. It is a process that should continue until they feel their global citizenship in the contemporary world.

Holistic education focuses on every aspect of a student’s development. It goes beyond academics to focus on their social, emotional, physical, and ethical development such that their learning outcomes give a more comprehensive view of their progress and growth. High school students in India will get many benefits if their school decides to adopt this holistic approach to their learning. Given below is a list of some benefits that a holistic approach to education holds for students: 1. The key to holistic development of students is ensuring students’ overall well-being. This is to say, in order for high school students in India to develop holistically, their overall well-being is of utmost concern. That includes their academic performance, interpersonal relationships (with parents and peers), emotional well-being, and ethical knowledge. The work of a holistic approach to education is to make this happen, and the role of teachers is immense in this work. 2. Students get to form a positive bond with their teachers. Rather than teachers becoming the sources of information, students on the way to attaining global citizenship in the contemporary world find that teachers have to transform into facilitators of knowledge in the holistic approach. This will help them form a positive bond with their teachers and reciprocate with an equal effort on their part. Holistic development of students comes from this bond that gets formed. Both the teachers and the students create an environment conducive to learning. Teachers have to take the lead initially, though. 3. A positive school environment that is full of project-based and self-directed learning takes birth. Project-based learning (PBL) is indispensable to holistic development, both in early childhood education and formal school education. When the teacher indulges students in this kind of learning that depends a lot on self-directed learning, students get to develop problem-solving skills and connect with the local and the global world around them. This connection helps students develop a sense of empathy and compassion for others, beneficial to the human race in the long run.

Benefits of Developing Reading Habits During Teen Years

Reading habits are crucial for personal growth, and building vocabulary during teenage years. Engaging in reading activities can help enhance cognitive abilities and offer numerous benefits for personal development. Reading is an essential skill that we all need in our daily lives. It is a way to learn, escape, and explore new perspectives. During our teenage years, we go through a lot of changes, and it is the perfect time to develop reading habits that can help us grow personally and intellectually. In this blog post, we will discuss the benefits of developing reading habits during teenage years.

How to develop reading habits during the teenage years:
1.Set aside time for reading every day. Reading at a particular hour everyday is highly beneficial as it helps develop more focus and concentration.
2.Choose books that interest you. You can also start with some comics, story novels etc. Find a quiet and comfortable space to read. Having a quiet place to read develops more interest and concentration.
3.Join a book club or reading group. Reading alone is good, but joining a reading group helps you explore other topics of interest as well. You can learn from other people and share your own learnings as well.
4.Use technology to access reading materials. The internet is full of reading materials. When it comes to reading, you must take full advantage of the resources available on the internet i.e., reading websites, blogs, group study websites, etc.
